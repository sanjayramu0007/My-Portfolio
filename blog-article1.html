On-page SEO

On-page SEO [also known as on-site SEO] refers to the practice of optimizing webpage to improve a website's search engine rankings and earn organic traffic. On-page SEO is important because it helps search engines understand your website and it's content as well as identify whether it is relevant to a search's query.

Its optimization of individual webpage content and HTML source code to help them rank higher in search results. It involves strategically placing keywordds, writing compelling titles and meta descriptions, using proper header tags, and ensure a fast, mobile-friendly user experience.

Create a high-quality, relevant content that matches the search intent of your audience. Place your keyword in the URL slug, the H1 heading, and a few H2/H3 subheadings, but avoid "keyword stuffing". Write click worthy descriptive titles & meta tags, organize your page structure comperss a image file sizes to improve page speed. Keep URLs clean, short and user- Friendly.

Tecnical SEO

Technical SEO involves optimizing your website's back-end infrastructure so search engines and AI systems can effciently crawl, render, index, and cite your content. Technical SEO refers to website and server optimizations that help search engines spiders and index your site more effectively [to help improve organic rankings]

Without a solid technical foundation, your best content won't appear in search results or get cited in AI-generated answers, no matter how valuanle it is. That means lost traffic, missed business opportunities, and fewer chances to be refernces when users turn to AI for answers. Make sure serach engines can discover(crawl) and save(index) your site properly so your pages can rank in search results. Check whether your site is indexed, as your site wont't show in search results if Google hasn't indexed it and to checking your indexing status using Google Search Console(GSC) and Bing Webmaster tools(BWT).

Having a duplicateversions of your site can harm your SEO efforts because search engines view these as separate websites, even though they display the same content. Use the HTTPS version as your primary URL then implement a 301 permanent redirect so users and search engines are forwarded to your preferred version.

Make sure your robots.txt file is accurately set up configure your "robots.txt" file to avoid blocking important pages from being crawled. A robots.txt file is a text files that tells search engine crawlers which parts of your site they're allowed to access.

Off-page SEO is the process of making high-quality backlinks for the website. off-page SEO refres to all actions taken outside of your website to improve its search engine rankings and overall online authority.
